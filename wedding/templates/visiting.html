Accomodations

Camping
There will be plenty of camping available at the wedding site for those who wish to stay for the weekend. Please contact us if you have any questions.

Transportation

If you are not planning to rent a car, please contact us for help getting to the wedding site so that we can help you get a ride with someone local. A cab ride to the wedding site would be cost prohibitive and we'll do what we can to make sure anyone to traveled from afar is well taken care of.

Things To Do

Alex and I love the Northwest and want to share our favorite places and activities with you even if we may not be able to show you around personally during your visit. The best part about living in Portland is the amazing natural beauty of the surrounding area, and the delicious food and beer available in town.

Oregon Coast

  • Canon Beach - more high end artsy community near an oregon coast postcard favorite "haystack rock." About 1:15 from Portland via Highway 26.
  • Seaside - very close to Canon beach, more traditional beachtown with all the touristy shops, family activities, and saltwater taffy. About 1:15 from Portland via Highway 26.
  • Ecola State Park - nice hike near Canon Beach and Seaside, there is a $5 day use fee to park. About 1:15 from Portland via Highway 26.
  • Astoria - more working class industrial beach town. Has a nice brewery (Ft George) and a beautiful bridge. You can take private glass blowing glasses and relive scenes from Goonies. About 1:30 from Portland via Highway 30.
  • Tillamook - it has a great 4 mile spit with an amazingly beautiful wide deserted beachfront that's perfect for a long walk. There's also the cheese factory, and a great fish and chip place right across from the factory (it doesn't look like much but it's food is fabulous). About 1:45 from Portland via Highway 26 - then take the cutoff to Tillamook.

Inland

  • Silver Falls - gorgeous 7 mile hike with 10 waterfalls, some of which you can walk behind. Start at the north rim, shorter loops are available.
  • Eagle Creek - Colette's favorite hike. Hike is on a cliff edge following a canyon river with amazing flourecent green moss and lush surroundings. 13 miles out and back, but almost no elevation gain, possible shallow stream fording.
  • Mount Hood Area
    • Lost Lake - beautiful lake with a great view of Mt Hood. Offers a very short hike, non-motorized boat rentals, great picnic spots with grills
    • Mirror Lake - short hike with a lake with a view of Mt Hood at the end, hike can be extended to Tom Dick and Harry peaks (still very doable day hike out and back).
    • Cooper Spur
    • HOod River
